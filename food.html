김밥(Kimbap)

Cooked rice, vegetables and any other ingrediants you prefer. You roll everything with gim(kim-dried sheets of seaweed). It's a popular dish when going on a picnic or quick meals during commute. Also it's not that difficult to make at home!

사진: Unsplash의Filippo Faruffini

김치(Kimchi)

Korean traditional side dish.
Many people know the cabbage kimchi but there are al sorts of kimchi such as cubed radish, cucumber, mustard leaf and so on. Instead of the commonly known cabbage kimchi you should definitely try other kinds too!

사진: Unsplash의Ra Dragon

비빔밥(Bibimbap)

Traditional Korean rice bowl.
A rice bowl usually topped with beef, sunny side up egg, vegetables and most importantly gochujang.
비빔 means mixing everything together and 밥 means rice; so basically it's a rice you eat after mixing all the ingredients together.

사진: Unsplash의Nikolay Smeh

비빔냉면(Bibim naengmyeon)

Cold, spicy noodles usually popular during summer. There might be chunks of ice in your bowl to make the noodles cooler. Asian pear is also the cherry on top. There's also an unspicy noodle with more water in it. Koreans have different opinions about them so try both!

사진: Unsplash의Crystal Jo

삼계탕(Samgyetang)

A kind of Chicken soup. Even though it's hot it's considered a summer health food. Also when mothers give bearth they eat this dish to maintain their health. There may be rice inside the chicken along with korean ginseng.

사진: Unsplash의Cyprien Delaporte

짜장면(Jajangmyeon)

Noodle with blackbean sauce.
Everyone's favorite dish. Usually cheap and delicious which makes it popular. Also the most populous place after childrens's sports day. There's an urban tradition of eating Jjajangmyeon after moving day.

사진: Unsplash의Ruth Georgiev

치킨(Fried chicken)

Korean fried chicken is coated with all kinds of sauces. Frying the chicken makes the chicken crunchy and the sauces are so good. It's a very popular dish among Koreans so you can see these stores on every block. Best with cold beer.

사진: Unsplash의Dylan Lu

삼겹살(Pork belly)

It literally means three layered pork. You can see in the picture that they are grilling the pork on the lid of a iron cauldron. Try grilling kimchi too!. Your clothes may smell greasy after, so check for storage space under your chair.

사진: Unsplash의Clint Bustrillos

순대(Sundae)

Type of blood sausage made by steaming cow or pig's intenstines stuffed with various ingredients. Usually eaten with Tteok-bokki.

사진: Unsplash의CJ Dayrit

빈대떡(Bindaetteok)

Mung bean batter with a filling made of braken pork, mung bean sprouts and cabbage kimchi. Eaten on national holidays like chuseok.

사진: Unsplash의Markus Winkler

떡볶이(Tteokbokki)

Traditional verson is made with rice cakes, fish cakes, and sometimes hard boiled eggs. It's a popular snack among school children.

사진: Unsplash의Markus Winkler

닭갈비(Dak-galbi)

Spicy stire-fried chicken famous in Chunchon. But you can see a lot of places in Seoul too. Usually served in a big cauldron.

사진: Unsplash의Yoonji kim

튀김(Fried food)

Any food that is fried. Shrimp, squid, sweet potato and practically anything. It's eaten as a side dish of tteok-bokki. I recommend dipping it in the tteok-bokki sauce. In some neighborhoods there's a truck where they sell this dish only.

사진: Unsplash의Markus Winkler

된장(Soybean paste)

Soybean paste is used in a variety of dishes. It's usually used in soybean soup but it can be used in all kinds of foods to season it. In the picture you can see it hanging out to dry this is a process of making the delicious paste.

사진: Unsplash의Junseong Lee

번데기(Pupa)

It's a traditional street food. Insect-based street food made with silkworm pupa. You might not see it on the street anymore as it's not as common as it was before. I've personally never tried it but you're free to try!

사진: Unsplash의Aigerim Kalysheva

닭발(Chicken feet)

This is another food I don't like but it's quite common here. It's literally chicken feet chopped at the ankle usually covered in spicy sauce. Be careful of the bones inside but some restaurants sell non-boned chicken feet which I prefer.

사진: Unsplash의BRALRO

돼지껍질(Pork rind)

It's the crispy skin of the pig. Because we Koreans eat anything, even the skin! When grilling it makes popping sounds as it is being fried. It's perfectly normal, just be careful not to let it fly away.

사진: Unsplash의BRALRO

제육볶음(Stir-fried spicy pork)

Spicy pork eaten with rice. Popular among all ages and sex. It's quite spicy so becareful! Some places may offer to make it less spicy. There is excessive sauce so make sure you mix it with the rice!

사진: Unsplash의Dynamic Wang

설렁탕(Sullungtang)

Milky beef bone soup that is made by boiling down ox leg bones for several hours until the broth becomes rich and creamy(personally it doesn't have much taste). Popular among the older ages.

사진: Unsplash의GRANT LEE

소주(Soju)

Clear and colorless Korean alcohol which is traditionally made by fertilizing rice. You can also drink it with mixing beer. With an average 18% if it's too strong for you try mixing it with soda!

사진: Unsplash의The Creativv

컵라면(Cup rameon)

Instant food that is sold in convenience stores. There are a variety of choices so try as much as you can! A lot of convenience stores have hot water you could use to cook and dispose of easily.

사진: Unsplash의sq lim

삼각김밥(Triangle Kimbap)

A popular choice to go with cup rameon is also sold in convenience stores. It's like kimbap but in a triangular shape and has meat or vegetables in the middle. Please try the tuna! It's my favorite :)

사진: Unsplash의Samia Liamani

빙수(Bingsu)

It is made by grinding frozen milk and adding fruit on top. The most authentic one would be red bean bingsu which is more traditional than the others. There are mango, strawberry, tiramisu and etc.

사진: Unsplash의Crystal Jo

시장(Market place)

Not a food but a great place to find one! A local market place will have the best foods and there's a lot to see besides that! I highly recommend going to some big market places nearby you.

사진: Unsplash의Mike Swigunski
